# Code of Conduct

Omnicanon serves texts that billions of people hold sacred. Contributors come from every tradition and from none. We hold ourselves to two standards:

**The usual open-source standard.** Be respectful and constructive. No harassment, personal attacks, or discrimination of any kind. Critique code and data, not people. (We follow the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).)

**A domain-specific standard.** Because this project touches religion:

- Treat every tradition's texts with scholarly respect, whether or not you share its beliefs.
- Keep discussions technical. Debates about which tradition is *true* are off-topic everywhere in this project.
- Proselytizing, mockery of any faith (or of non-belief), and sectarian polemics in issues, PRs, code, or data will result in removal.
- Corpus disputes (variant readings, versification differences, translation choices) are legitimate technical discussions — argue them with sources, not conviction.

Report violations privately to the maintainer via GitHub. Consequences range from a warning to a permanent ban at maintainer discretion.
